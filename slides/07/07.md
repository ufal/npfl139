# Continuous Action Space

# Continuous Action Space

Until now, the actions were discrete. However, many environments naturally
accept actions from a continuous space. We now consider actions which come
from range $[a, b]$ for $a, b вҲҲ в„қ$, or more generally from a Cartesian product
of several such ranges:
$$вҲҸ_i \big[a_i, b_i\big].$$

~~~
![w=40%,f=right](normal_distribution.svgz)
A simple way how to parametrize the action distribution is to employ
the normal distribution.

~~~
Given mean $Ој$ and variance $Пғ^2$, probability density function of $рқ“қ(Ој, Пғ^2)$
is
$$p(x; Ој, Пғ^2) вүқ \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 ПҖ Пғ^2}} e^{\large-\frac{(x - Ој)^2}{2Пғ^2}}.$$

---
# Continuous Action Space in Gradient Methods

Utilizing continuous action spaces in gradient-based methods is straightforward.
Instead of the categorical distribution with the $\softmax$ activation, we
produce a continuous action distribution, usually the normal distribution.

~~~
Considering only one real-valued action, we therefore have
$$ПҖ(a | s; вҶ’Оё) вүқ P\Big(a вҲј рқ“қ\big(Ој(s; вҶ’Оё), Пғ(s; вҶ’Оё)^2\big)\Big),$$
where $Ој(s; вҶ’Оё)$ and $Пғ(s; вҶ’Оё)$ are function approximation of mean and standard
deviation of the action distribution.

~~~
The mean and standard deviation are usually computed from the shared
representation, with
- the mean being computed as a usual regression (i.e., one output neuron
  without activation);
~~~
- the standard deviation (which must be positive) being computed again as
  a single output, but with either $\exp$ or $\operatorname{softplus}$
  activation, where $\operatorname{softplus}(x) вүқ \log(1 + e^x)$.

---
# Continuous Action Space in Gradient Methods

During training, we compute $Ој(s; вҶ’Оё)$ and $Пғ(s; вҶ’Оё)$ and then sample the action
value (clipping it to $[a, b]$ if required). To compute the loss, we utilize
the probability density function of the normal distribution (and usually also
add the entropy penalty).

~~~
```python
  mus_layer = torch.nn.LazyLinear(actions)
  sds_layer = torch.nn.LazyLinear(actions)
  ...
  mus = mus_layer(hidden_layer)
  sds = sds_layer(hidden_layer)
  sds = torch.exp(sds)   # or sds = torch.nn.softplus(sds)

  action_dist = torch.distributions.Normal(mus, sds)

  # Loss computed as - log ПҖ(a|s) * returns - entropy_regularization
  loss = - action_dist.log_prob(actions) * returns \
         - args.entropy_regularization * action_dist.entropy()
```

---
# Continuous Action Space

When an action consists of several real values, i.e., the action is a suitable
subregion of $в„қ^n$ for $n>1$, we can:
- either use multivariate Gaussian distribution, or
~~~
- factorize the probability into a product of univariate normal
  distributions.
~~~
  - This is the most commonly used approach; we then consider the action
    to be composed of several independent **action components**.

~~~
If modeling the action distribution using a unimodal normal distribution is
insufficient, a mixture of normal distributions (or a mixture of logistic) can
be used, capable of representing also multimodal distributions.

~~~
However, such an approach is more common in generative modeling; if we believe
the optimal policy is greedy, a unimodal distribution is usually enough.

# Deterministic Policy Gradient Theorem

Combining continuous actions and Deep Q Networks is not straightforward.
In order to do so, we need a different variant of the policy gradient theorem.

~~~
Recall that in policy gradient theorem,
$$вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} J(вҶ’Оё) вҲқ рқ”ј_{s вҲј Ој} \Big[вҲ‘\nolimits_{a вҲҲ рқ“җ} q_ПҖ(s, a) вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(a | s; вҶ’Оё)\Big].$$

~~~
## Deterministic Policy Gradient Theorem
Assume that the policy $ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё)$ is deterministic and computes
an action $aвҲҲв„қ$.
~~~
Further, assume the reward $r(s, a)$ is actually
a deterministic function of the given state-action pair.
~~~
Then, under several assumptions about continuousness, the following holds:
$$вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} J(вҶ’Оё) вҲқ рқ”ј_{s вҲј Ој} \Big[вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё) вҲҮ_a q_ПҖ(s, a)\big|_{a=ПҖ(s;вҶ’Оё)}\Big].$$

The theorem was first proven in the paper Deterministic Policy Gradient Algorithms
by David Silver et al. in 2014.

---
# Deterministic Policy Gradient Theorem вҖ“ Proof

The proof is very similar to the original (stochastic) policy gradient theorem.

~~~
However, we will be exchanging derivatives and integrals, for which we need
several assumptions:
~~~
- we assume that $h(s), p(s' | s, a), вҲҮ_a p(s' | s, a), r(s, a), вҲҮ_a r(s, a),
  ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё), вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё)$ are continuous in all parameters and variables;
~~~
- we further assume that $h(s), p(s' | s, a), вҲҮ_a p(s' | s, a), r(s, a), вҲҮ_a
  r(s, a)$ are bounded.

Details (which assumptions are required and when) can be found in Appendix B
of the paper _Deterministic Policy Gradient Algorithms: Supplementary Material_ by
David Silver et al.

---
# Deterministic Policy Gradient Theorem вҖ“ Proof

$\displaystyle вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} v_ПҖ(s) = вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} q_ПҖ(s, ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё))$

~~~
$\displaystyle \phantom{вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} v_ПҖ(s)} = вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё}\Big(r\big(s, ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё)\big) + вҲ«_{s'} Оіp\big(s'| s, ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё)\big)\big(v_ПҖ(s')\big) \d s'\Big)$

~~~
$\displaystyle \phantom{вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} v_ПҖ(s)} = вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё) вҲҮ_a r(s, a) \big|_{a=ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё)} + вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} вҲ«_{s'} Оіp\big(s' | s, ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё)\big) v_ПҖ(s') \d s'$

~~~
$\displaystyle \phantom{вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} v_ПҖ(s)} = вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё) вҲҮ_a \Big(r(s, a) + вҲ«_{s'} Оіp\big(s' | s, a\big) v_ПҖ(s') \d s' \Big) \Big|_{a=ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё)}\\
                    \qquad\qquad\qquad + вҲ«_{s'} Оіp\big(s' | s, ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё)\big) вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} v_ПҖ(s') \d s'$

~~~
$\displaystyle \phantom{вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} v_ПҖ(s)} = вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё) вҲҮ_a q_ПҖ(s, a)\big|_{a=ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё)} + вҲ«_{s'} Оіp\big(s' | s, ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё)\big) вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} v_ПҖ(s') \d s'$

~~~
We finish the proof as in the gradient theorem by continually expanding $вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} v_ПҖ(s')$, getting
$вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} v_ПҖ(s) = вҲ«_{s'} вҲ‘_{k=0}^вҲһ Оі^k P(s вҶ’ s'\textrm{~in~}k\textrm{~steps~}|ПҖ) \big[вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(s'; вҶ’Оё) вҲҮ_a q_ПҖ(s', a)\big|_{a=ПҖ(s';вҶ’Оё)}\big] \d s'$
~~~
and then $вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} J(вҶ’Оё) = рқ”ј_{s вҲј h} вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} v_ПҖ(s) вҲқ рқ”ј_{s вҲј Ој} \big[вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё) вҲҮ_a q_ПҖ(s, a)\big|_{a=ПҖ(s;вҶ’Оё)}\big]$.

# Deep Deterministic Policy Gradients

Note that the formulation of deterministic policy gradient theorem allows an
off-policy algorithm, because the loss functions no longer depend on actions
sampled from the behavior policy (similarly to how expected Sarsa is also an
off-policy algorithm).

~~~
We therefore train function approximation for both $ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё)$ and $q(s, a; вҶ’Оё)$,
training $q(s, a; вҶ’Оё)$ using a deterministic variant of the Bellman equation,
$$q(S_t, A_t; вҶ’Оё) = рқ”ј_{S_{t+1}} \big[r(S_t, A_t) + Оі q(S_{t+1}, ПҖ(S_{t+1}; вҶ’Оё))\big],$$
and $ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё)$ according to the deterministic policy gradient theorem.

~~~
The algorithm was first described in the paper Continuous Control with Deep Reinforcement Learning
by Timothy P. Lillicrap et al. (2015).

~~~
The authors utilize a replay buffer, a target network (updated by exponential
moving average with $П„=0.001$), batch normalization for CNNs, and perform
exploration by adding an Ornstein-Uhlenbeck noise to the predicted actions.
Training is performed by Adam with learning rates of 1e-4 and 1e-3 for the
policy and the critic networks, respectively.

# Ornstein-Uhlenbeck Exploration

While it is natural to use Gaussian noise for the exploration policy, the
authors claim that temporally-correlated noise is more effective for physical
control problems with inertia.

~~~
They therefore generate noise using Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process, by computing
$$n_t вҶҗ n_{t-1} + Оё вӢ… (Ој - n_{t-1}) + ОөвҲјрқ“қ(0, Пғ^2),$$
utilizing hyperparameter values $Оё=0.15$ and $Пғ=0.2$.

# Twin Delayed Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient

The paper Addressing Function Approximation Error in Actor-Critic Methods by
Scott Fujimoto et al. from February 2018 proposes improvements to DDPG, which:

~~~
- decrease maximization bias by training two critics and choosing the minimum of
  their predictions;

~~~
- introduce several variance-lowering optimizations:
  - delayed policy updates;
  - target policy smoothing.

~~~

In 2022, together with the SAC algorithm, the TD3 algorithm has been one of the
best algorithms for off-policy continuous-actions RL training.

---
style: .katex-display { margin: 0 }
# TD3 вҖ“ Maximization Bias

Similarly to Q-learning, the DDPG algorithm suffers from maximization bias.
In Q-learning, the maximization bias was caused by the explicit $\max$ operator.
For DPG methods, it can be caused by the gradient descent itself (which also
maximizes, the negative loss).

~~~
Let us have a parametric critic $q_{вҶ’ПҶ}$ and a policy $ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё}$.
~~~
Consider two different DPG update results:
~~~
- $ПҖ_\textit{approx}$, using the parametric critic $q_{вҶ’ПҶ}$, in the direction $вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё) вҲҮ_a q_{вҶ’ПҶ}(s, a)\big|_{a=ПҖ(s;вҶ’Оё)}$,
~~~
- $ПҖ_\textit{true}$, using the (unavailable) real critic $q_ПҖ$, in the direction $вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё) вҲҮ_a q_ПҖ(s, a)\big|_{a=ПҖ(s;вҶ’Оё)}$.
~~~

Because the gradient direction is a local maximizer, for sufficiently small
$Оұ<Оө_1$ we have
$$рқ”ј\big[q_{вҶ’ПҶ}(s, ПҖ_\textit{approx})\big] вүҘ рқ”ј\big[q_{вҶ’ПҶ}(s, ПҖ_\textit{true})\big].$$

~~~
However, for real $q_ПҖ$ and for sufficiently small $Оұ<Оө_2$, it holds that
$$рқ”ј\big[q_ПҖ(s, ПҖ_\textit{true})\big] вүҘ рқ”ј\big[q_ПҖ(s, ПҖ_\textit{approx})\big].$$

~~~
Therefore, if $рқ”ј\big[q_{вҶ’ПҶ}(s, ПҖ_\textit{true})\big] вүҘ рқ”ј\big[q_ПҖ(s, ПҖ_\textit{true})\big]$,
for $Оұ < \min(Оө_1, Оө_2)$
$$рқ”ј\big[q_{вҶ’ПҶ}(s, ПҖ_\textit{approx})\big] вүҘ рқ”ј\big[q_ПҖ(s, ПҖ_\textit{approx})\big].$$

---
# TD3 вҖ“ Maximization Bias

![w=50%](td3_bias.svgz)![w=50%](td3_bias_dqac.svgz)

~~~
Analogously to Double DQN we could compute the learning targets using
the current policy and the target critic, i.e., $r + Оі q_{вҶ’Оё'}(s', ПҖ_{вҶ’ПҶ}(s'))$
(instead of using the target policy and the target critic as in DDPG), obtaining DDQN-AC algorithm.
However, the authors found out that the policy changes too slowly and the target
and current networks are too similar.

~~~
Using the original Double Q-learning, two pairs of actors and critics could be
used, with the learning targets computed by the opposite critic, i.e.,
$r + Оі q_{вҶ’Оё_2}(s', ПҖ_{вҶ’ПҶ_1}(s'))$ for updating $q_{вҶ’Оё_1}$. The resulting DQ-AC
algorithm is slightly better, but still suffering from overestimation.

---
# TD3 вҖ“ Algorithm

The authors instead suggest to employ two critics and one actor. The actor is
trained using one of the critics, and both critics are trained using the same
target computed using the _minimum_ value of both critics as
$$r + Оі \min_{i=1,2} q_{вҶ’Оё'_i}(s', ПҖ_{вҶ’ПҶ'}(s')).$$
The resulting algorithm is called CDQ вҖ“ Clipped Double Q-learning.

~~~
Furthermore, the authors suggest two additional improvements for variance
reduction.
- For obtaining higher quality target values, the authors propose to train the
  critics more often. Therefore, critics are updated each step, but the actor
  and the target networks are updated only every $d$-th step ($d=2$ is used in
  the paper).

~~~
- To explicitly model that similar actions should lead to similar results,
  a small random noise is added to the performed actions when computing the
  target value:
  $$r + Оі \min_{i=1,2} q_{вҶ’Оё'_i}(s', ПҖ_{вҶ’ПҶ'}(s') + Оө)~~~\textrm{for}~~~
    Оө вҲј \operatorname{clip}(рқ“қ(0, Пғ), -c, c),~\textrm{with}~Пғ=0.2, c=0.5.$$

# Soft Actor Critic

The paper _Soft Actor-Critic: Off-Policy Maximum Entropy Deep Reinforcement
Learning with a Stochastic Actor_ by Tuomas Haarnoja et al. from Jan 2018
introduces a different off-policy algorithm for continuous action space
(i.e., not relying on the DPG theorem).

~~~
It was followed by a continuation paper _Soft Actor-Critic Algorithms and
Applications_ in Dec 2018.

~~~
The general idea is to introduce entropy directly in the value function we want
to maximize, instead of just ad-hoc adding the entropy penalty. Such an approach
is an instance of _regularized policy optimization_.

---
# Soft Actor Critic Objective

Until now, our goal was to optimize
$$рқ”ј_ПҖ \big[G_0\big].$$

~~~
Assume the rewards are deterministic and that $Ој_ПҖ$ is on-policy distribution of
a policy $ПҖ$.

In the soft actor-critic, the authors consider infinite-horizon MDPs and propose
to optimize the maximum entropy objective
$$\begin{aligned}
ПҖ_* &= \argmax_ПҖ рқ”ј_{sвҲјОј_ПҖ} \Big[рқ”ј_{aвҲјПҖ(s)}\big[r(s, a)\big] + Оұ H(ПҖ(вӢ…|s))\Big] \\
    &= \argmax_ПҖ рқ”ј_{sвҲјОј_ПҖ, aвҲјПҖ(s)}\big[r(s, a) - Оұ \log ПҖ(a|s)\big].
\end{aligned}$$

~~~
Note that the value of $Оұ$ is dependent on the magnitude of returns and that
for a fixed policy, the entropy penalty can be вҖңhiddenвҖқ in the reward.

---
# Soft Actor Critic Objective

To maximize the regularized objective, we define the following augmented reward:
$$r_ПҖ(s, a) вүқ r(s, a) + рқ”ј_{s' вҲј p(s, a)} \big[Оұ H(ПҖ(вӢ…|s'))\big].$$

~~~
From now on, we consider **soft action-value** function corresponding to this
augmented reward.

~~~
Note that compared to the previous slide, the entropy is computed in the next
state $s'$, not in the current state $s$; the paper does not comment on this
change.

# Soft Policy Evaluation

---
# Soft Policy Evaluation

Our goal is now to derive **soft policy iteration**, an analogue of policy iteration algorithm.

~~~
We start by considering soft policy evaluation. Let a modified Bellman backup
operator $рқ“Ј_ПҖ$ be defined as
$$рқ“Ј_ПҖ q(s, a) вүқ r(s, a) + Оі рқ”ј_{s' вҲј p(s, a)} \big[v(s')\big],$$
where the **soft (state-)value** function $v(s)$ is defined as
$$
v(s) = рқ”ј_{a вҲј ПҖ} \big[q(s, a)\big] + ОұH(ПҖ(вӢ…|s)) = рқ”ј_{a вҲј ПҖ} \big[q(s, a) - Оұ \log ПҖ(a|s)\big].$$

~~~
This modified Bellman backup operator corresponds to the usual one for the
augmented rewards $r_ПҖ(s, a)$, and therefore the repeated application
$рқ“Ј_ПҖ^k q$ converges to $q_ПҖ$ according to the original proof.

# Soft Policy Improvement

---
# Soft Policy Improvement

While the soft policy evaluation was a straightforward modification of the
original policy evaluation, the soft policy improvement is quite different.

~~~
Assume we have a policy $ПҖ$, its action-value function $q_ПҖ$ from the soft
policy evaluation, and we want to improve the policy.
~~~
We show that the improved policy is proportional to $e^{\frac{1}{Оұ} q_ПҖ(s, a)}$.

~~~
Furthermore, in function approximation we have to select
the improved policy from a family of parametrized distributions $О $.
~~~
We therefore define the improved policy $ПҖ'$ as
$$ПҖ'(вӢ…|s) вүқ \argmin_{ПҖМ„ вҲҲ О } J_ПҖ(ПҖМ„) вүқ \argmin_{ПҖМ„ вҲҲ О } D_\textrm{KL}\Bigg( ПҖМ„(вӢ…|s) \Bigg\| \frac{\exp\big(\frac{1}{Оұ} q_ПҖ(s, вӢ…)\big)}{z_ПҖ(s)} \Bigg),$$
where $z_ПҖ(s)$ is the partition function (i.e.,  normalization factor such that
the right-hand side is a distribution), which does not depend on the new policy
and thus can be ignored.

---
# Soft Policy Improvement

We now prove that $q_{ПҖ'}(s, a) вүҘ q_ПҖ(s, a)$ for any state $s$ and action $a$.

~~~
We start by noting that $J_ПҖ(ПҖ') вүӨ J_ПҖ(ПҖ)$, because we can always choose $ПҖ$ as
the improved policy.
~~~
Therefore,
$$рқ”ј_{aвҲјПҖ'} \big[Оұ\log ПҖ'(a|s) - q_ПҖ(s, a) + \textcolor{gray}{Оұ\log z_ПҖ(s)}\big] вүӨ
  рқ”ј_{aвҲјПҖ} \big[Оұ\log ПҖ(a|s) - q_ПҖ(s, a) + \textcolor{gray}{Оұ\log z_ПҖ(s)}\big],$$

~~~
which results in
$$рқ”ј_{aвҲјПҖ'} \big[q_ПҖ(s, a) - Оұ\log ПҖ'(a|s)\big] вүҘ v_ПҖ(s).$$

~~~
We now finish the proof analogously to the original one:
$$\begin{aligned}
q_ПҖ(s, a) &= r(s, a) + Оірқ”ј_{s'}[v_ПҖ(s')] \\
          &вүӨ r(s, a) + Оірқ”ј_{s'}[рқ”ј_{a'вҲјПҖ'} [q_ПҖ(s', a') - Оұ\log ПҖ'(a'|s')] \\
          &вҖҰ \\
          &вүӨ q_{ПҖ'}(s, a).
\end{aligned}$$

---
# Soft Policy Iteration

The soft policy iteration algorithm alternates between the soft policy
evaluation and soft policy improvement steps.

~~~
The repeated application of these two steps produces better and better policies.
In other words, we get a monotonically increasing sequence of soft action-value
functions.

~~~
If the soft action-value function is bounded (the paper assumes
a bounded reward and a finite number of actions to bound the entropy), the
repeated application converges to some $q_*$, from which we get a $ПҖ_*$ using
the soft policy improvement step.

~~~
It remains to show that the $ПҖ_*$ is indeed the optimal policy
fulfilling $q_{ПҖ_*}(s, a) вүҘ q_ПҖ(s, a)$.

~~~
However, this follows from the fact that at convergence,
$J_{ПҖ_*}(ПҖ_*) вүӨ J_{ПҖ_*}(ПҖ)$, and following the same reasoning as in the proof of
the soft policy improvement, we obtain the required $q_{ПҖ_*}(s, a) вүҘ q_ПҖ(s, a)$.

---
# Soft Policy Improvement Derivation

The following derivation is not in the original paper, but it is my
understanding of how the softmax of the action-value function arises.
For simplicity, we assume finite number of actions, but the same
approach can be generalized to continuous actions.

~~~
Assuming we have a policy $ПҖ$ and its action-value function $q_ПҖ$,
we usually improve the policy using
$$\begin{aligned}
  ОҪ(вӢ…|s)
  &= \argmax_ОҪ рқ”ј_{aвҲјОҪ(вӢ…|s)} \big[q_ПҖ(s, a)\big] \\
  &= \argmax_ОҪ вҲ‘\nolimits_a q_ПҖ(s, a) ОҪ(a|s) \\
  &= \argmax_ОҪ вҶ’q_ПҖ(s, вӢ…)^\T вҶ’ОҪ(вӢ…|s), \\
\end{aligned}$$

~~~
which results in a greedy improvement with the form of
$$ОҪ(s) = \argmax\nolimits_a q_ПҖ(s, a).$$

---
# Soft Policy Improvement Derivation

Now consider instead the regularized objective
$$\begin{aligned}
  ОҪ(вӢ…|s)
  &= \argmax_ОҪ \big( рқ”ј_{aвҲјОҪ(вӢ…|s)} \big[q_ПҖ(s, a)\big] + ОұH(ОҪ(вӢ…|s))\big) \\
  &= \argmax_ОҪ \big(рқ”ј_{aвҲјОҪ} \big[q_ПҖ(s, a) - Оұ \log ОҪ(a|s)\big]\big)
\end{aligned}$$

~~~
To maximize it for a given $s$, we form a Lagrangian
$$рқ“ӣ = \Big(вҲ‘\nolimits_a ОҪ(a|s) \big(q_ПҖ(s, a) - Оұ\log ОҪ(a|s)\big)\Big) - О»\Big(1 - вҲ‘\nolimits_a ОҪ(a|s)\Big).$$

~~~
The derivative with respect to $ОҪ(a|s)$ is
$$\frac{вҲӮрқ“ӣ}{вҲӮОҪ(a|s)} = q_ПҖ(s, a) - Оұ\log ОҪ(a|s) - Оұ + О».$$

~~~
Setting it to zero, we get $Оұ\log ОҪ(a|s) = q_ПҖ(s, a) + О» - Оұ$, resulting in $ОҪ(a|s) вҲқ e^{\frac{1}{Оұ} q_ПҖ(s, a)}$.

# Soft Actor Critic Algorithm

Our soft actor critic will be an off-policy algorithm with continuous action
space. The model consists of two critics $q_{вҶ’Оё_1}$ and $q_{вҶ’Оё_2}$, two target
critics $q_{вҶ’ОёМ„_1}$ and $q_{вҶ’ОёМ„_2}$, and a single actor $ПҖ_{вҶ’ПҶ}$.

~~~
The authors state that
- with a single critic, all the described experiments still converge;

~~~
- they adopted the two critics from the TD3 paper;
~~~
- using two critics вҖңsignificantly speed up trainingвҖқ.

---
# Soft Actor Critic вҖ“ Critic Training

To train the critic, we use the modified Bellman backup operator, resulting in
the loss
$$J_q(вҶ’Оё_i) = рқ”ј_{sвҲјОј_ПҖ, aвҲјПҖ_{вҶ’ПҶ}(s)} \Big[\big(q_{вҶ’Оё_i}(s, a) - \big(r(s, a) + Оі рқ”ј_{s' вҲј p(s, a)} [v_\textrm{min}(s')]\big)\big)^2\Big],$$

~~~
where
$$v_\textrm{min}(s) = рқ”ј_{aвҲјПҖ_{вҶ’ПҶ}(s)} \Big[\min_i\big(q_{вҶ’ОёМ„_i}(s, a) \big) - Оұ \log ПҖ_{вҶ’ПҶ}(a | s)\Big].$$

~~~
The target critics are updated using exponential moving averages with
momentum $П„$.

---
# Soft Actor Critic вҖ“ Actor Training

The actor is updated by directly minimizing the KL divergence, resulting in the
loss
$$J_ПҖ(вҶ’ПҶ) = рқ”ј_{sвҲјОј_ПҖ, aвҲјПҖ_{вҶ’ПҶ}(s)}\Big[Оұ \log\big(ПҖ_{вҶ’ПҶ}(a | s)\big) - \min_i\big(q_{вҶ’Оё_i}(s, a)\big)\Big].$$

~~~
Given that our critics are differentiable, in order to be able to compute the
gradient $вҲҮ_{вҶ’ПҶ} q_{вҶ’Оё_i}(s, a)$, we only need to reparametrize the policy as
$$a = f_{вҶ’ПҶ}(s, Оө).$$

~~~
Specifically, we sample $Оө вҲј рқ“қ(0, 1)$ and let $f_{вҶ’ПҶ}$ produce an unbounded
Gaussian distribution $рқ“қ\big(Ој(s; вҶ’ПҶ), Пғ(s; вҶ’ПҶ)^2\big)$, or a diagonal one if
the actions are vectors, with the sampled action $a = Ој(s; вҶ’ПҶ) + ОөПғ(s; вҶ’ПҶ)$.

~~~
Together, we obtain
$$J_ПҖ(вҶ’ПҶ) = рқ”ј_{sвҲјОј_ПҖ, ОөвҲјрқ“қ(0, 1)}\Big[Оұ \log\big(ПҖ_{вҶ’ПҶ}(f_{вҶ’ПҶ}(s, Оө), s)\big) - \min_i\big(q_{вҶ’Оё_i}(s, f_{вҶ’ПҶ}(s, Оө))\big)\Big].$$

---
# Soft Actor Critic вҖ“ Bounding Actions

In practice, the actions need to be bounded.

~~~
The authors propose to apply an invertible squashing function $\tanh$
on the unbounded Gaussian distribution.

~~~
Consider that our policy produces an unbounded action $ПҖ(u | s)$.
To define a distribution $ПҖМ„(a | s)$ with $a = \tanh(u)$, we need to employ
the change of variables, resulting in
$$ПҖМ„(a | s) = ПҖ(u | s) \bigg(\frac{вҲӮa}{вҲӮu}\bigg)^{-1} = ПҖ(u | s) \bigg(\frac{вҲӮ\tanh(u)}{вҲӮu}\bigg)^{-1}.$$

~~~
Therefore, the log-likelihood has quite a simple form
$$\log ПҖМ„(a | s) = \log ПҖ(u | s) - \log\big(1 - \tanh^2(u)\big).$$

---
# Soft Actor Critic вҖ“ Automatic Entropy Adjustment

One of the most important hyperparameters is the entropy penalty $Оұ$.

~~~
In the second paper, the authors presented an algorithm for automatic adjustment
of its value.

~~~
Instead of setting the entropy penalty $Оұ$, they propose to specify target
entropy value $рқ“—$ and then solve a constrained optimization problem
$$ПҖ_* = \argmax_ПҖ рқ”ј_{sвҲјОј_ПҖ, aвҲјПҖ(s)} \big[r(s, a)\big]\textrm{~~such that~~}рқ”ј_{sвҲјОј_ПҖ, aвҲјПҖ(s)}\big[-\log ПҖ(a | s)\big] вүҘ рқ“—.$$

~~~
We can then form a Lagrangian with a multiplier $Оұ$
$$рқ”ј_{sвҲјОј_ПҖ, aвҲјПҖ(s)} \Big[r(s, a) + Оұ\big(-\log ПҖ(a | s) - рқ“—\big)\Big],$$
which should be maximized with respect to $ПҖ$ and minimized with respect
to $Оұ вүҘ 0$.

---
# Soft Actor Critic вҖ“ Automatic Entropy Adjustment

To optimize the Lagrangian, we perform _dual gradient descent_, where we
alternate between maximization with respect to $ПҖ$ and minimization with respect
to $Оұ$.

~~~
While such a procedure is guaranteed to converge only under the convexity
assumptions, the authors report that the dual gradient descent works in practice
also with nonlinear function approximation.

~~~
To conclude, the automatic entropy adjustment is performed by introducing
a final loss
$$J(Оұ) = рқ”ј_{sвҲјОј_ПҖ, aвҲјПҖ(s)} \big[-Оұ \log ПҖ(a | s) - Оұ рқ“—\big].$$
